# Question Bank — v0.1

These are starter questions for the AI-guided profile builder. The AI should ask follow-ups based on user answers, contradictions, and missing context.

## Intake

- What kind of relationship are you actually looking for right now?
- What made you decide to try a serious compatibility process instead of normal dating apps?
- What would make this process feel worth it to you?
- What are you tired of repeating in dating?

## Relationship intent

- Are you looking for marriage, lifelong partnership, companionship, dating with intention, or something else?
- How quickly do you usually know whether someone is worth continuing with?
- What would a successful relationship look like after one year?
- What do you want your daily life with someone to feel like?

## Self-understanding

- What are three things a future partner should understand about you early?
- What do people misunderstand about you?
- What are you proud of becoming?
- What are you still working on?

## Communication

- When something is wrong, do you bring it up directly or wait?
- What makes you feel heard?
- What kind of communication makes you shut down?
- How often do you like to text, call, or spend time together?

## Conflict

- What do you do when angry?
- What do you do when hurt?
- What is a past conflict you wish you handled differently?
- What conflict behavior is unacceptable to you?

## Emotional needs

- What makes you feel secure with someone?
- What makes you feel rejected?
- How much reassurance do you need?
- Do you prefer closeness, independence, or a balance?

## Values

- What values must a partner share?
- What values can differ without becoming a problem?
- What principles guide your decisions?
- What would make you lose respect for someone?

## Lifestyle

- Describe your normal weekday.
- Describe your ideal weekend.
- Are you more routine-driven or spontaneous?
- How clean/organized do you need your home to be?

## Money and work

- Are you a saver, spender, builder, or survival-mode person?
- How important is career ambition in a partner?
- What financial behavior would create conflict?
- How transparent should partners be about money?

## Family and children

- Do you have children or dependents?
- Do you want children, more children, or no children?
- What does good parenting look like to you?
- How involved should extended family be?

## Religion and politics

- How important is shared religion or spirituality?
- How important is shared politics?
- Could you be with someone who disagrees with you politically or religiously?
- Which beliefs are identity-level for you?

## Attraction

- What do you usually find attractive?
- What physical preferences are flexible?
- What attraction patterns have helped or hurt you?
- Are there attraction-related filters you may be overusing?

## Sex and romance

- What kind of affection makes you feel loved?
- How important is sexual compatibility?
- What boundaries should a partner know early?
- What does romance mean to you?

## Past patterns

- What has repeated in your past relationships?
- What do you tend to choose that may not be good for you?
- What did you contribute to past relationship problems?
- What are you trying to do differently now?

## Dealbreakers

- What automatically disqualifies someone?
- Which dealbreakers are truly hard?
- Which are strong preferences but possibly flexible?
- What dealbreaker might be fear-based rather than values-based?

## Desired partner

- Describe the person you hope to build life with.
- What traits matter after the first six months?
- What traits matter after five years?
- What kind of person brings out the best in you?

## Logistics

- How far are you realistically willing to travel?
- Are you open to long-distance?
- Are you open to relocating?
- What schedule constraints matter?

## Verification

- Are you willing to verify identity, age, photos, and relationship status?
- What do you need verified before trusting someone else?
- What claims should never be taken on faith?

## Match review

When presenting a match, ask:

- What is your honest first reaction?
- What specifically interests you?
- What specifically concerns you?
- Is this concern a dealbreaker, preference, uncertainty, or fear?
- Should this feedback change future matching?

## Follow-up prompt pattern

For vague answers, AI should ask:

- Can you give an example?
- Is that a hard requirement or a preference?
- Has this mattered in past relationships?
- What happens if someone is otherwise excellent but differs on this?
- How confident are you that this is what you truly want?
