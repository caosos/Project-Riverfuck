# Profile Categories

This document defines the first structured profile map for Project Riverfuck.

The system should not rely on shallow bios. It should build a rich compatibility profile through AI-guided conversation, structured questions, verification, and user review.

## Category map v0.1

### 1. Identity and basics

Purpose: establish basic context without reducing the person to demographics.

Example fields:

- legal/preferred name
- age/date of birth
- gender identity
- sexual orientation
- relationship intent
- location and relocation openness
- language(s)
- cultural background
- relationship status
- children / dependents

Example questions:

- What name do you want people to use?
- What kind of relationship are you seeking?
- Are you open to relocation, long-distance, or only local matches?
- Do you have children or dependents? How should a partner fit into that reality?

### 2. Relationship goals

Purpose: determine what kind of relationship the user actually wants.

Fields:

- casual vs serious intent
- marriage interest
- cohabitation interest
- timeline urgency
- monogamy / non-monogamy expectations
- children/family goals
- desired level of independence

Questions:

- What would a successful relationship look like one year from now?
- Are you seeking marriage, lifelong partnership, companionship, dating, or something else?
- What are you unwilling to compromise on in a relationship?

### 3. Values and worldview

Purpose: detect deep alignment or conflict.

Fields:

- religion/spirituality
- politics/civic worldview
- ethics
- honesty expectations
- family values
- service/community orientation
- tolerance for difference

Questions:

- What values must a partner share with you?
- Which values can differ without becoming a problem?
- What moral or lifestyle differences would make a relationship impossible?

### 4. Communication style

Purpose: evaluate how people talk, listen, repair, and handle tension.

Fields:

- direct vs indirect communication
- texting/calling preferences
- conflict repair style
- emotional expressiveness
- need for reassurance
- listening style
- apology style

Questions:

- When you are upset, do you want space, discussion, reassurance, or action?
- How do you prefer a partner bring up a problem?
- What communication habits make you feel respected?

### 5. Conflict and stress behavior

Purpose: identify compatibility under pressure, not just when everything is easy.

Fields:

- anger style
- avoidance tendencies
- shutdown/escalation patterns
- problem-solving style
- stress triggers
- coping methods
- accountability patterns

Questions:

- What do you do when overwhelmed?
- What is a past relationship conflict you handled badly, and what did you learn?
- What kind of conflict behavior is unacceptable to you?

### 6. Emotional patterns and attachment

Purpose: understand closeness, independence, trust, security, and vulnerability.

Fields:

- attachment tendencies
- jealousy/insecurity patterns
- emotional availability
- affection needs
- trust-building pace
- vulnerability comfort
- reassurance needs

Questions:

- What makes you feel secure in a relationship?
- What makes you pull away?
- Do you need frequent reassurance, or do you prefer independence?

### 7. Daily lifestyle

Purpose: determine day-to-day compatibility.

Fields:

- sleep schedule
- work schedule
- cleanliness/organization
- hobbies
- social frequency
- introvert/extrovert tendencies
- routine vs spontaneity
- homebody vs going out

Questions:

- Describe your normal weekday.
- Describe your ideal weekend.
- What daily habits would drive a partner crazy?

### 8. Health, wellness, and limitations

Purpose: understand practical realities without stigmatizing people.

Fields:

- major health constraints user chooses to disclose
- disability/accessibility needs
- fitness/activity level
- diet
- sleep
- substance boundaries
- mental health management

Questions:

- Are there health or lifestyle realities a serious partner should understand?
- What does taking care of yourself look like?
- Are there substances, habits, or health behaviors that are dealbreakers?

### 9. Work, ambition, and money

Purpose: evaluate practical life compatibility.

Fields:

- employment situation
- ambition level
- work-life balance
- income expectations
- debt comfort
- spending/saving habits
- financial transparency expectations
- generosity/frugality

Questions:

- How important is career ambition to you in a partner?
- Are you a saver, spender, builder, or survival-mode person financially?
- What money behaviors would create serious conflict?

### 10. Family and children

Purpose: prevent major life-plan mismatches.

Fields:

- existing children
- desire for children
- parenting style
- co-parenting realities
- family involvement
- elder care expectations
- holiday/family traditions

Questions:

- Do you want children, more children, or no children?
- What does good parenting look like to you?
- How involved should extended family be in your life?

### 11. Religion, politics, and culture

Purpose: explicitly handle high-conflict areas instead of hiding them.

Fields:

- must-match beliefs
- tolerated differences
- political intensity
- religious practice intensity
- cultural expectations
- holiday/religious practices

Questions:

- Could you be with someone who disagrees politically?
- Could you be with someone who practices a different faith or no faith?
- Which beliefs are identity-level for you?

### 12. Attraction and physical preferences

Purpose: capture attraction honestly without letting it dominate the whole process.

Fields:

- physical preferences
- age range
- body type preferences
- grooming/style preferences
- attraction flexibility
- non-negotiable attraction factors

Questions:

- What do you usually find attractive?
- Where are your preferences flexible?
- Have your attraction patterns helped or hurt your long-term relationship goals?

### 13. Sexual and romantic compatibility

Purpose: prevent hidden mismatch in romance and intimacy expectations.

Fields:

- affection style
- sexual values
- libido compatibility
- romance expectations
- boundaries
- exclusivity expectations
- public/private affection

Questions:

- What kind of affection makes you feel loved?
- How important is sexual compatibility to you?
- What boundaries should a partner understand early?

### 14. Past relationship patterns

Purpose: learn from history without turning the profile into a confessional.

Fields:

- relationship history themes
- breakup causes
- lessons learned
- repeated patterns
- accountability
- unresolved concerns

Questions:

- What pattern has repeated in your past relationships?
- What did you contribute to past relationship failures?
- What are you trying to do differently now?

### 15. Dealbreakers and hard constraints

Purpose: clearly separate hard requirements from preferences.

Fields:

- hard dealbreakers
- soft dealbreakers
- safety boundaries
- lifestyle non-negotiables
- moral non-negotiables
- location/logistics hard limits

Questions:

- What automatically disqualifies someone?
- Which dealbreakers are truly hard, and which are preferences?
- Which past dealbreaker might be worth revisiting if compatibility is otherwise strong?

### 16. Preferences and ideals

Purpose: define desired partner without confusing fantasy with reality.

Fields:

- preferred traits
- desired lifestyle
- desired emotional style
- preferred communication
- shared activities
- growth goals

Questions:

- Describe the person you hope to build life with.
- What traits matter most after the first six months?
- What traits have you overvalued in the past?

### 17. Practical logistics

Purpose: ensure real-world feasibility.

Fields:

- distance limits
- transportation
- work schedule
- custody schedule
- relocation
- availability
- timing

Questions:

- How far are you realistically willing to travel for a relationship?
- How often could you see someone?
- What schedule constraints would a partner need to understand?

### 18. Verification and trust signals

Purpose: reduce fraud, misrepresentation, and wasted time.

Fields:

- identity verification
- photo/video verification
- relationship status verification
- employment/education optional verification
- social proof optional
- background checks optional

Questions:

- Which parts of your profile are you willing to verify?
- What do you need verified before trusting someone else?

### 19. AI-observed traits

Purpose: derived model observations from conversation, not claims.

Fields:

- consistency
- communication clarity
- emotional regulation indicators
- openness
- rigidity/flexibility
- contradiction markers
- seriousness score
- completion depth

Important: these must be treated as model observations, not facts.

### 20. Match readiness

Purpose: determine whether a user is ready to be matched or needs more profile work.

Fields:

- profile completeness
- contradiction resolution
- unresolved dealbreakers
- emotional readiness
- intent clarity
- safety readiness

Questions:

- Is this user clear enough to match responsibly?
- Are there contradictions that must be resolved first?
- Are they seeking a person or chasing an impossible checklist?

## Category notes

The system should distinguish:

- hard constraints
- strong preferences
- flexible preferences
- attraction filters
- fear-based rejection
- values mismatch
- logistics mismatch
- unknown/missing data

The AI interviewer should not simply accept every stated preference as equally valid. It should help classify and challenge them when appropriate.
